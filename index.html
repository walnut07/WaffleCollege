This is the story of how I started to wonder if I could work as a software engineer

She thought engineering was too difficult for her

She got into college of social science though she had such a strong interest in Technology.


Since she was a kid, she had loved creating something by using a computer; she composed songs by playing instruments and programming on music software. Even a professional musician described her song as “this song has a similar vibe to Matsutoya Yumi.”
Though she loved composing songs with Tech, she thought STEM subjects were too difficult for her to major in. This is assumingly because almost all of her friends were going to major in Humanities or Social Science as well.

She decided to go to women’s college to study social welfare and gender studies which she had been curious about since she thought women were excluded in society.

Yet, college sparkled
her interest
in Tech

When she took statistics and algorithm classes, she found herself desiring to know more about computer science.
She self-taught Python for 2-3 hours every day even out of classes.

Gradually, she started to dream of working as a software engineer.
Yet, she initially thought “I’ll get stuck for sure because I haven’t even learned highschool-level math. No experience at all.”

On the other hand, there was part of her who thought “I’d certainly regret my decision if I gave up here.” She denied to study STEM without reasonable reasons. She would make the same mistake if she made a decision while underrating herself and ingoring her career vision.

She made up her mind to leave college for a year so that I can focus more on learning programming.
