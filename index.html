This is the story of how I started to wonder if I could work as a software engineer

She thought STEM were too difficult for her

She got into a Humanities college though she had such a strong interest in Technology.


Since she was a kid, she had loved creating something by using a computer; she composed songs by playing real instruments and programming on music software. One time, even a professional musician described her song as “this song has a similar vibe to Matsutoya Yumi.”
Though she loved composing songs with Tech, she thought STEM subjects were too difficult for her to major in. This is assumingly because almost all of her friends were going to major in Humanities as well.

She decided to go to a women’s college to major in social welfare and gender studies which she had been curious about since she thought women were underrated in society.

Yet, college sparkled
her interest
in Tech

When she took statistics and algorithm classes, she found herself desiring to know more about computer science.
She self-taught Python for 2-3 hours every day even out of classes.

Gradually, she started to dream of working as a software engineer.
Yet, she had thought “I’ll get stuck for sure because I haven’t even learned highschool-level math. No experience at all.”

On the other hand, there was part of her who thought “I’d certainly regret my decision if I gave up here.” To make decisions by underrating herself and not considering her career vision is exactly what she did when she decided to major in Humanities.

She made up her mind to leave college for a while so that I will be able to hunt for a job as a software engineer.
